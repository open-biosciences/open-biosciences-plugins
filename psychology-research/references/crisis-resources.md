# Crisis Resources

This file is included verbatim into every psychology-research skill's safety preamble at sync time. If user input or analysis indicates acute self-harm, suicide risk, active abuse, psychiatric crisis, or imminent danger, name these resources, encourage immediate use, and pause analytical work.

## United States — General

- **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline** — call or text 988
- **SAMHSA National Helpline** (mental health and substance use, 24/7) — 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
- **Crisis Text Line** — text HOME to 741741

## Population-specific

- **Veterans Crisis Line** — 988, then press 1; or text 838255
- **The Trevor Project** (LGBTQ+ youth) — 1-866-488-7386 or text START to 678-678
- **Trans Lifeline** — 877-565-8860
- **RAINN** (sexual assault) — 1-800-656-HOPE (4673)
- **Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline** — 1-800-422-4453

## Emergency

- **911** for immediate life-threatening emergencies. Note that police involvement in mental-health crises has known risks for marginalized communities (Black, Indigenous, disabled, neurodivergent, trans, unhoused). Where available locally, mobile crisis teams or co-response units may be safer first contacts; users should be informed of the option and supported in choosing.

## Outside the United States

The plugin currently bundles US-centric crisis resources. International users should consult their country's equivalent crisis services. The IASP (International Association for Suicide Prevention) maintains a directory at iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres. This is a Tier-N coverage gap to be addressed.
